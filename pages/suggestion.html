Insights & Recommendations

1. Expand Bays in High-Demand Suburbs on a Limited Scale

Insight: High-demand suburbs like Potts Point and Darlinghurst show consistent booking needs.

Recommendation: Start with a limited increase in bays in these areas, focusing on a small number of high-traffic locations. Rather than deploying entirely new infrastructure one should assess the feasibility of reallocating underused bays from low-demand suburbs to these areas which will help reducing setup costs.

2. Seasonal Resource Adjustment with Flexible Bay Use

Insight: Usage data shows seasonal trends with increased demand during warmer months.

Recommendation: Implement a flexible strategy (like align, adapt and amplify) where additional bays are temporarily assigned to high-demand areas during peak seasons. Collaborate with operators to arrange temporary expansions, as needed, without committing to permanent increases. costs.

3. Test Time-Based Pricing in Limited High-Demand Bays

Insight: Peak hours and weekdays show consistently high usage.

Recommendation: Introduce time-based pricing as a pilot program in a few high-demand bays. Offer lower prices during off-peak hours to encourage more balanced usage throughout the day. Gather data from this pilot to assess user response and operational feasibility before wider implementation.

4. Strengthen Partnerships with High-Performing Operators on Existing Contracts

Insight: Some operators achieve higher booking volumes, indicating strong customer preference.

Recommendation: Strengthen collaboration with top-performing operators by exploring opportunities within existing contracts. Instead of restructuring contracts give more importance to incentives (e.g., performance bonuses) to motivate higher engagement in high-demand areas.

5. Predictive Maintenance on Bays with Established High Demand

Insight: High-traffic bays require consistent maintenance to prevent service disruptions.

Recommendation: Application of predictive maintenance schedules only to bays with consistently high demand. Regular maintenance checks should mainly focus on these locations first, which helps keep operational costs low by limiting this program to the most critical bays.

6. Targeted Marketing for Underutilized Bays Without Additional Costs

Insight: Under utilised bays may suffer from lack of awareness or lower demand.

Recommendation: Run a digital marketing campaign aimed at residents in low utilisation areas, using existing city social media channels and newsletters to avoid additional costs. Highlight benefits of car sharing to drive local interest without needing to reallocate or increase resources.

7. Launch Commuter Programs with Minimal Adjustments

Insight: Longer travel distances on weekdays suggest commuter reliance on car sharing.

Recommendation: Introduce commuter-focused subscription or loyalty programs that reward frequent weekday use. Structure this as an add-on to existing offerings, keeping implementation straightforward and minimizing changes to operations.

8. Focus Bay Placement Near Major Public Transit Hubs as a Long-Term Goal

Insight: Bays near public transport hubs show high usage due to convenience for multi transport users.

Recommendation: While adding bays near transport hubs may require infrastructure planning, prioritize future bay expansion in these areas based on strategic transport routes. Collaborate with transport agencies (like Opal, etc.) for phased rollouts to keep implementation costs manageable.

9. Implement Long-Distance Trip Promotions as Digital-Only Initiatives

Insight: Certain suburbs serve users with longer-distance travel needs.

Recommendation: Offer digital promotions targeted at suburbs with high long-distance use (e.g., email or app notifications). This approach requires minimal operational changes and leverages existing communication channels indeed making it cost-effective and easy to deploy.

10. Pilot Distance-Based Pricing for Long-Distance Trips in Select Areas

Insight: Longer trip distances could justify a distance-based pricing model.

Recommendation: Conduct a limited pilot in high-distance areas to test user response to distance-based pricing. This can help gauge the impact on both revenue and demand without a full-scale implementation, reducing initial operational complexities.

11. Regular Bay Status Updates via Digital Channels for User Awareness

Insight: Maintaining operational status in high-demand bays is crucial for user satisfaction.

Recommendation: Use the car-sharing app or website to update users on bay availability and maintenance schedules. This ensures users are informed in real-time without requiring additional on-site staff or infrastructure, keeping updates efficient and low-cost.

12. Relocate Only the Most Underutilized Bays

Insight: Some bays show persistently low utilization in specific areas.

Recommendation: Relocate only a small number of the lowest-utilized bays to high-demand areas. Conduct this in phases, moving one or two bays at a time to assess the impact on utilization before making larger changes, thus keeping disruption and costs minimal.

13. Encourage Off-Peak Usage Through Non-Monetary Incentives

Insight: Certain times show significantly lower usage rates.

Recommendation: To increase off-peak usage, offer non-monetary incentives such as loyalty points for bookings during these times. This approach avoids revenue loss from discounted rates while still encouraging usage during quieter hours.

14. Highlight Environmental Impact to Foster Program Support

Insight: Car sharing reduces emissions and traffic, appealing to eco-conscious residents.

Recommendation: Share environmental impact statistics (e.g., emissions saved, reduced congestion) through annual reports, social media, and newsletters. Highlighting these benefits can boost public perception and support for the program without requiring operational changes.
